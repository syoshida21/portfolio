About Me

Stephanie Yoshida is a current senior at Punahou School in Hawaii. Born in Bangkok, Thailand, she has since lived in Honolulu for the past 17 years. Stephanie is an invested astronomy researcher focusing on searching for planets outside of the Solar System and a dedicated musician who has been playing the violin for the past 12 years. At Punahou School, Stephanie is the vice president of the Key Club and a member of the Helping Oncology Patients Everywhere Club. In her free time, she enjoys picnicking on the beach with her friends, watching Major League Baseball games (Go Braves!), and spending time with her family and pets. She also enjoys thoroughly listening to hours and hours of music on her Spotify playlists and traveling in order to immerse herself in different cultures!

Astronomy

My Research

Over the past four years, Stephanie has completed two research projects centralized around exoplanets--planets that orbit stars outside of the Solar System. She has been working alongside her mentor, Dr. Samuel Grunblatt, a postdoctoral fellow at the American Museum of Natural History in New York. Stephanie's astronomy research journey began the summer before her freshman year when she participated in the University of Hawaii's Hi-Star program. Following the completion of Hi-Star, she began her search for white dwarf exoplanets. By utilizing data from the Kepler/K2 telescope, Las Cumbres Observatory, and Southern Astrophysical Research Telescope, she came across a potential white dwarf eclipsing binary system, EPIC251248385. She has received numerous accolades for her findings and has published her work in the Research Notes of the American Astronomical Society journal. Beginning in her junior year, Stephanie shifted her research focus to looking for planets orbiting stars of extragalactic origin. With data from the TESS and Gaia observatories, she calculated the potential occurrence rate for these types of planets. She hopes to publish her work in the coming months. In addition to her research, Stephanie established an after school outreach program at a local elementary school, Hokulani, named "Beyond Our Stars" which she hosted in the spring semester of 2020. The goal of Beyond Our Stars was to teach young students about the fundamental of astronomy in the hopes of inspiring a new generation of scientists. She intended to continue the program, however, due to the coronavirus pandemic, sessions were halted.

Music

Stephanie began playing the violin at the age of six under the tutelage of Mrs. Judy Barrett. She has been a member of the Punahou Orchestra program since 5th grade and is now a first violinist in the Punahou Symphony Orchestra. In 2020, Stephanie was selected as one of five soloists for the Punahou Concerto Competition. Stephanie is also a part of the Punahou Chamber Music program where she is currently the first violinist in the Vaught String Quartet and has been a member of the Cooke and Watanabe String Quartets as well as the Chang Piano Quintet. For the past 10 years, Stephanie has participated in the Hawaii Youth Symphony and has most recently been in the Hawaii Youth Symphony I. In addition to this, Stephanie has spent her summers attending music camps across the country. In 2017, she spent two weeks at the Marrowstone Music Festival in Bellingham, Washington. In 2018, she took part in the Encore Chamber Music Institute at the Cleveland Institute of Music working with violinist Jinjoo Cho and faculty at CIM.
